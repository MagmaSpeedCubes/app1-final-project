Urban Issues

Challenges caused by rapid urban growth

As cities expand rapidly, poor planning can lead to environmental damage, transportation problems, and reduced quality of life.

Traffic Congestion

Urban expansion increases car use, leading to traffic jams, longer commutes, and higher pollution levels.

Habitat Loss

City expansion replaces natural ecosystems with buildings and roads, reducing biodiversity and green space.

Urban Heat Islands

Concrete and asphalt trap heat, making cities significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas.

Why it matters

Urban growth has measurable environmental consequences.

Cities are expanding faster than ever, and the data shows clear impacts: higher emissions, increased heat exposure, and growing strain on infrastructure and public health systems.
